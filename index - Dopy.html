Movie Review: Joker

Joaquin Phoenix returns to the screen as Arthur Fleck in Joker directed and written by Todd Phillips. This dramatic crime film centers in on the mental, physical, and emotional health of people that are struggling with everyday life. As Arthur Fleck dodges through the obstacles in his life his mental health begins to deteriorate and eventually he snaps while on a train thus starting his career of crime and terror.

this film is one of the more realistic DC films as it brings a lot of real world issues to this fictional Joker character, this allows the viewer to be more engaged in the movie. In this film Phoenix goes through about everything; Love (obsession), family issues, embarrassment, rage, and obviously escaping the law. I say obsession because of a couple pivotal scenes in which Fleck seems to be dreaming or fantasizing about situations he would want to be in. On the screen it looks like he is actually doing these things however the viewers find out shortly that they were just day dreams. I will admit that for a moment I also thought that Fleck was finding love really easily and that he was called on to the stage on the Murray Franklin show. I was very caught off guard when I realized they were merely day dreams.

Theories made by fans also exist stating that the majority of this movie is just Fleck imagining things thus making all of the events dreams. I mean the story does begin to go off of the rails when Fleck get invited on to the Murray Franklin show, followed by Fleck murdering a fellow co-worker (only one), followed then by the Murray Franklin incident a couple nights later. We may never know if this film was a reality for Fleck or just a mere dream. Does everything go crazily right for Arthur Fleck or is he simply plotting against randall, Murray Franklin, and even his own mother (not Gary though). I give Joker by Todd Phillips a 10/10 rating due to the complexity and emotion that was given to this previously evil and mentally disturbed DC villain. I would not recommend this movie for children however if you are a teen or adult I think that this is a very interesting watch, and maybe if you begin to feel empathy for Fleck...then you can begin to unlock YOUR inner Joker - O'KeefeGeek
